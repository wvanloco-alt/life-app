## Design Context

### Users

A small, closed group of trusted friends — not the general public. The primary user is the developer himself. Everyone using this app is invited; there is no public signup. Users open the app daily, usually in the morning or evening, to plan their week, log activities, track their budget, and reflect on their goals. The context is **quiet and intentional** — they are not rushing, they are being deliberate.

The job to be done: turn long-term values and goals into daily action, without anxiety or noise.

### Brand Personality

**Three words**: Calm. Grounded. Premium.

**Voice**: Like a trusted advisor who speaks plainly and doesn't oversell. Understated confidence. Never cheerleads, never gamifies, never nags.

**Tagline**: "Put First Things First" — a direct reference to Stephen Covey's 7 Habits. This is the north star.

**Emotional goal**: The user should feel *clear-headed and capable* after opening the app — not motivated in a shallow way, but genuinely oriented. The interface should feel like a well-made notebook, not a dashboard.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary references**:
- **Notion** — calm, structured, editorial hierarchy. Generous whitespace. Typography does the heavy lifting.
- **Things 3** — warm, intentional, personal. Not clinical. Feels like it was made for *you*, not for a company.

**Anti-references** (what this must never feel like):
- Aggressive fitness apps (Strava, MyFitnessPal) — loud, streak-obsessed, gamified
- Generic SaaS dashboards — cold blue palettes, metric cards stacked to infinity
- Hustle-culture productivity tools (Notion templates for "crushing your goals") — anxious, performance-driven
- Corporate enterprise software — sterile, dense, no soul

**Visual tone**: Editorial minimalism with warmth. Think printed matter — a well-designed annual report or a quality journal, not a startup landing page.

**Color**: Warm amber OKLCH neutrals throughout (hue ~45–55). No cool grays, no blue tints. Primary background in light mode: `oklch(0.98 0.004 55)` — like warm paper. Dark mode is a deep warm charcoal, not black.

**Typography**:
- Display/headings: **Fraunces** — a variable optical-size serif. Used for page titles, card headers, the sidebar brand name. Signals editorial quality and care.
- Body: **Plus Jakarta Sans** — clean, warm, legible. Not Inter. Not system default.
- Code/data: **JetBrains Mono** — only where precision matters (activity metrics, budget numbers in tabular context).

**Radius**: `0.625rem` — subtle. Avoids sharp edges without being pill-shaped. Cards feel solid, not bubbly.

**Motion**: `fade-up` entrance animations with `ease-out-quart` easing (300ms). Staggered reveals on lists. Never bouncy. `prefers-reduced-motion` always respected.

**Default theme**: Light. The warm paper-like background is the intended first impression.

### Design Principles

1. **Calm over stimulation.** If something could be noisier or quieter, choose quieter. Reduce the number of things competing for attention on any screen.

2. **Typography carries meaning.** Use Fraunces for anything that deserves weight — section names, goal titles, key metrics. Use weight and size contrast to create hierarchy; avoid decorative elements to compensate for weak structure.

3. **Data is personal, not performative.** This is not a public leaderboard. Numbers are shown to help the user reflect, not to impress. Don't use progress bars that "look like achievements." Keep charts clean and readable.

4. **Whitespace is not wasted space.** The app is used by one person who knows it well. It does not need to explain itself on every screen. Generous spacing signals confidence and calm.

5. **Warm neutrals only.** Every color decision should stay in the warm amber family or use the defined palette colors (`--palette-green`, `--palette-amber`, etc.) purposefully. Never introduce cool grays, blues, or purples as neutral tones.
