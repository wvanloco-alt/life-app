About the book
In "Winning Ugly," Olympic gold medalist and ESPN analyst
Brad Gilbert delivers essential strategies to help players
outsmart and outmaneuver even their toughest opponents.
Now in its third edition, this tennis classic includes a fresh
introduction featuring insights from modern champions like
Roger Federer, Novak Djokovic, Serena Williams, and Andy
Murray. Gilbert shares invaluable tips and techniques that
have earned acclaim from both recreational and professional
players alike. With a blend of wit and clarity, this
comprehensive guide equips readers with the mental and
tactical tools needed to prevail on the court, solidifying its
status as a definitive resource for tennis enthusiasts around the
globe.

About the author
Brad Gilbert is a former professional tennis player, renowned
coach, and insightful author whose experience in the sport has
earned him a reputation as a strategic thinker and an expert in
mental toughness. Born on August 9, 1963, in Oakland,
California, Gilbert rose to prominence in the tennis world
during the late 1980s and early 1990s, achieving a career-high
ATP singles ranking of No. 15 and securing numerous titles.
However, it is his post-playing career as a coach, notably for
top players like Andre Agassi and Andy Roddick, that has
solidified his legacy in the sport. In his acclaimed book
"Winning Ugly," Gilbert distills his unique approach to tennis,
emphasizing the psychological aspects of competition and the
importance of playing to one's strengths, making the work an
essential read for athletes and enthusiasts alike.

The Early Edge: The Match Begins Before the

Mental Preparation: The Pre-Match Advantage
In this section, Brad Gilbert emphasizes the importance of
mental preparation before a tennis match. He reflects on his
early career experiences, learning that top players began their
mental preparation long before stepping onto the court.
Gilbert notes that many players, including himself, often
arrived at matches unprepared, leading to slow starts and

missed opportunities.
Start Your Match Before It Begins
Gilbert advocates for a proactive approach to match
preparation. Mental warm-ups should begin during the trip to
the court, focusing on evaluating the opponent and
visualizing successful shots. He stresses that mental
readiness is crucial for a successful match, as most players
neglect this aspect.
The Pre-Match Mental Checklist
A robust mental checklist involves reviewing previous
encounters, shot tendencies, and the opponent’s game style.
Gilbert also emphasizes understanding the psychological
elements of the opponent's game, such as their emotional
reactions during matches. This strategic mental planning sets
the foundation for a successful game plan.
The Game Plan
Pre-match strategy should be clear and focused. Gilbert
outlines a few key questions to determine your game plan:

1. What do I want to make happen?
2. What do I want to prevent from happening?
Incorporating these reflections into the match preparation
helps solidify a player's approach based on the opponent’s
strengths and weaknesses.
Set Your Compass
A mental compass is vital for navigating the match’s
challenges. By outlining strategies and preparing mentally,
players can maintain focus amid the rapid developments
during the match, crucial for responding effectively to
surprises.
Preparation on Tour: Becker and Connors
Gilbert uses his experiences against opponents Boris Becker
and Jimmy Connors to illustrate different strategies. Against
Becker, Gilbert focuses on exploiting Becker’s weaknesses
and maintaining composure when faced with aggression.
Against Connors, he reflects on managing crowd dynamics
and utilizing slicing tactics.
Pre-Match Preparation Pays Off

When Gilbert finally implemented his pre-match strategies,
he began winning matches. His notable comeback against
Becker at the U.S. Open showcases the significance of
mental preparation and adaptability during difficult match
situations.
Every Player Is Unique, But Connors Is the Most
Unique!
Gilbert contrasts his match strategy against Connors with that
against Becker. Against Connors, he needs to manage crowd
control and use slice shots effectively, while the strategy for
Becker emphasizes controlling the pace during rallies.
Your Mind Can’t Think of Two Things at Once
Entering the match, players often feel nervous, which can
hinder performance. Gilbert presents his "Nerve Busters" that
include focused breathing, maintaining good footwork,
reading the ball label during the warm-up, and using
distractions like singing to keep nerves in check.
Ready for the Contest

With mental and physical preparation in place, Gilbert
emphasizes the importance of making a strong start to the
match. Early leads can significantly increase the chances of
winning, given that players who win the first set have high
odds of winning the match overall.
Start Smart: Grabbing the Early Lead
Gilbert encourages players to seize early opportunities within
the first two games. He advises choosing to receive serve
first and emphasizes smart serving, avoiding unforced errors,
and maintaining focus during the initial games. The first
changeover serves as an opportunity for mental regrouping
and reflection on the game plan.
Overall, Gilbert's insights highlight the crucial link between
mental preparation, strategic planning, and physical readiness
for athletes looking to enhance their performance on the
tennis court.

Playing Smart: Finding a Way to Win

The Key to Victory
Brad Gilbert discusses his tactical approach to tennis,
emphasizing the importance of leveraging one’s strengths
and targeting opponents’ weaknesses. He believes success
stems from understanding the dynamics of the match and
exploiting the opponent's vulnerabilities while minimizing
their advantages.
Who’s Doing What to Whom?
Understanding the flow of the game involves always
assessing who is winning points and why. Awareness of the
match's dynamics helps players adapt and strategize
effectively against their opponents.

The Combination to the Lock
Every player has distinct strengths and weaknesses, akin to a
combination lock. Identifying these allows a player to unlock
opportunities during matches. Gilbert shares how studying
opponents’ games led him to success, urging players to scout
their rivals for their vulnerabilities.
Fat Fritz and My Little Black Book
Gilbert shares a personal anecdote about losing to Fritz
Buehning due to insufficient tactical preparation. After that,
he began documenting strengths and weaknesses of
opponents in a “little black book,” which became integral to
his strategy for future matches.
The Tennis Lie Detector: Pressure
Pressure reveals a player’s true capabilities. Gilbert stresses
the importance of recognizing and exploiting weak shots that
opponents inevitably resort to under stressful conditions.
Agassi at First Glance

Studying players like Andre Agassi highlights the
importance of power and timing in returns. Agassi’s
adeptness at early ball striking demonstrates how aggressive
play can dominate opponents.
Be a Good Scout
Coupling observation with tactical knowledge can
significantly uplift one’s game. Players should note
opponents’ tendencies and weaknesses during matches to
refine their strategies.
Know Thyself (And Thy Tennis Game)
Gilbert encourages players to honestly assess their game
strengths and weaknesses, seeking constructive feedback
from coaches or peers to devise more effective tactical
approaches.
Drawing the Wrong Conclusions
Overconfidence or insecurity can distort perceptions of
opponents. Gilbert discusses the importance of starting each
match with a fresh mindset, reminding players to focus on

their abilities rather than past performance.
The Combination to Krickstein’s Lock
Taking strategic action during pivotal moments is crucial.
Gilbert shares a personal match against Aaron Krickstein,
where he successfully exploited Krickstein's weaknesses by
adjusting his approach.
Destroying Your Opponent’s Game Plan
Understanding common opponent types—like Retrievers,
Serve-Volley Players, and powerful servers—helps in
developing specific strategies to counter their strengths and
exploit their weaknesses.
Advanced Strategies for Overcoming Challenges
1.
Retrievers
: Employ patience, approach the net, and force them into
uncomfortable positions.
2.
Players with Speed

: Utilize breaking patterns and force them to play under
pressure by keeping shots deep and targeting middle court.
3.
Backhand Attacks
: Target weaknesses with strategic consistency, particularly
with conservative play under pressure.
4.
Serving
: Focus on placement and consistency, rather than just power,
especially on second serves.
5.
Closing Matches
: Maintain mental composure when leading, avoiding
complacency.
Learning from Legends
Gilbert examines the playing styles of tennis legends such as
Andre Agassi, Ivan Lendl, Boris Becker, John McEnroe, and
Jimmy Connors, emphasizing lessons to incorporate into
personal play. He underscores the importance of mental
toughness, adaptability, and aggressive tactics in enhancing
one’s game.

Basic Brad
Key principles from Gilbert focus on minimizing unforced
errors, remaining observant during matches, playing
strengths to opponents’ weaknesses, and nurturing mental
discipline throughout competitive scenarios.
In conclusion, maximizing strengths, minimizing
weaknesses, and cultivating mental awareness are
foundational to winning in tennis. By understanding
dynamics and adapting strategies, players can navigate match
challenges effectively.

1.Question
How can mental preparation before a match give you an
advantage?
Answer:Mental preparation allows players to
visualize their performance, analyze their
opponents' strengths and weaknesses, and formulate
a specific game plan. This proactive approach
enables them to enter the match focused and ready,
rather than relying on getting into the rhythm
during the first few games, which can lead to a
significant disadvantage.
2.Question
What does it mean to start your match before it actually
starts?
Answer:Starting your match before it begins refers to the
concept of mental and physical preparation that happens

prior to stepping onto the court. This includes evaluating the
opponent during practice, planning strategies, and getting
mentally primed to seize opportunities. Players should begin
mentally preparing on their way to the match to ensure they
are in the right mindset from the very first point.
3.Question
What is the significance of evaluating your opponent
before the match?
Answer:Evaluating your opponent allows you to identify
their best and weakest shots, understand their playing style,
and prepare counters for their strategies. This insight helps in
customizing your game plan and boosts confidence, creating
an early mental edge.
4.Question
What should your immediate focus be during the first few
games of the match?
Answer:Your immediate focus during the first few games
should be to minimize unforced errors, establish consistency,
and build a rhythm without overextending yourself. This

helps set the tone for the match and builds a solid foundation
to work from.
5.Question
How can you use the changeover to your advantage?
Answer:The first changeover is an opportunity to mentally
recalibrate and reinforce your strategy, focusing on what you
intend to make happen and what to avoid. It serves as a
moment to collect thoughts, assess performance, and plan
adjustments before the match progresses.
6.Question
What are some techniques to manage nerves before and
during a match?
Answer:Managing nerves can be achieved through deep,
controlled breathing, conscious footwork to remain light and
agile, focusing on specific tasks such as reading the ball's
label, and even singing under your breath to shift focus from
anxiety. These techniques help center your thoughts on
performance rather than the pressure of competition.
7.Question
Why is it important to play your weaker shots in the

warm-up?
Answer:Utilizing your weaker shots during the warm-up
helps build confidence and prepares you for the match. This
practice ensures that you don’t neglect those strokes during
the game, strengthening them over time and improving your
overall play.
8.Question
How does the choice to serve or receive first impact the
match start?
Answer:Choosing to receive first can provide an early
psychological edge, as it places pressure on your opponent to
serve well from the start, often leading to mistakes. It also
allows you to warm up into the match rhythm while your
opponent is navigating their initial serve, which is typically
less effective.
9.Question
What should players visualize the night before a match?
Answer:Players should visualize successful plays against
their opponent, recalling past matches to predict what shots

will be effective and mentally rehearsing how they will
respond to different situations. This mental rehearsal sets a
positive tone for the upcoming competition.
10.Question
What contributes to developing a winning mindset before
a match?
Answer:A winning mindset develops through thorough
mental preparation, knowledge of one's own strengths and
weaknesses, awareness of the opponent's game, and
maintaining a positive attitude towards potential outcomes.
Being mentally primed creates resilience and composure
during the match.

and slow down their own tempo as a counter-strategy. It’s
essential to protect one's own rhythm and not get flustered by
the opponent's tactics.
