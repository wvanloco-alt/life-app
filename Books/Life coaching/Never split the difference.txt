Be a mirror


Mirroring is when you repeat the last 3 words of a person's sentence, or the most important on of the last 3. We fear what is different and are drawn to what is similar
This to trigger the mirror instinct and your counterpart will enevitably elaborate on what was just said and sustain the process of connecting


* Smile while talking
* Mind the sound and tone of your voice
* Being right isn't the key to a succesfull negotiation
* Instead of doing any thinking at all in the early goings about what your going to say, make your sole purpose the other person and what they are going to say
* Make it about the other person, validating their emotions and creating trust


How to confront ( and get your way ) without confrontation


1. Talk in a low downward voice 
2. Start with: "I'm sorry ..."
3. Mirror
4. Silence, at least 4 seconds, let the mirror do it's work
5. Repeat


Every time you mirror someone, they will reword what they said. Ask someone "what do you mean by that?"  and you will likely encounter irritation or defensiveness


* Use your skills to reveal surprises you are certain to find
* Don't commit to assumptions, but use the negotiation to test them rigurously
* Negotiation is not an act of battle, it's a process of discovery
* Slow it down
* SMILE - Positivity creates mental agility for you and your counterpart


Don't feel their pain, label it




Showing empathy is the basis of healthy human interaction. try to understand the other person's point of view and don't mistake empathy with simpathy
Human connection is the first goal. Sprinkle this in every conversation you have.


Tactical empathy


* Imagine yourself in your counterpart's situation. Acknowledge the other person's situation. Once they know that you are listening, they may tell you somethin you can use
* Focus first on clearing the barriers of agreement
* Pause. After you label a barrier or mirror a statement, let it sink in
* Label you counterpart's fears to diffuse their power. The faster you interrupt action in your counterpart's amygdala ( brain's fear cnter ), the faster you can generate feeling of safety, well-being and trust
* Empathy is a powerful mood enhancer
* List the worst things the other person could say about you and say them before the other person can. Speaking them might encourage the other person to claim they are not true
* You ar edealing with a person who wants to be appreciated and understood. So use labels to reïnforce and encourage positive perceptions and dynamics


Beware YES - Master NO


Using these tool often proves difficult because it goes against "being nice". Making a person feel safe and in control can be achieved by getting them to disagree, to draw their own boundaries and define thier desires.


* Break the habit of attempting to get people to say YES. Our push for YES makes people defensive. Our love for hearing YES makes us blind
* NO is not a failure. It often just means "wait", or "I'm not comfortable with that". Learn how to hear it calmly, it is not the end but the beginning
* YES is the final goal, don't aim for it at the start
* Saying NO makes the speaker feel safe and in control, so trigger it. ( "stoor ik?" Is beter dan "kunnen we even praten?" )
* Sometimes you have to force them into a NO, even if it means intentionally mis-labeling one of their emotions
* Negotiate in their world. It's abount the other party convincing themselves that the solution yoy want is their idea.

Bend their reality


These rules apply because they recognize the human psyche as it is. There is a pattern in our emotional and irrational behavior


   * All negotiations are defined by subterranean desires and needs, don't let yourself be fooled by the surface
   * Splitting the difference is wearing ,one black and one brown shoe, so don't compromise. Meeting halfway often leads to bad deals for both sides.
   * The word FAIR is often used to exploit people on the defensive and gain concessions. Don't get suckered in, instead, ask them to explain how you are mistreating them
   * Bend their reality by setting extreme anchors, making the real offer seem more reasonable
   * People take more risk to avoid a loss than to realize a gain


Create the illusion of control


Who has control, the guy listening or the guy talking? The listener of course


   * Don't try to force your opponent to admit that you are right. Aggressve confrontation is the enemy of constructive communication
   * Avoid questions that can be answered with YES or tiny pieces of information
   * Ask calibrated that questions that start with "hoe" of "wat". By implicitely asking your counterpart for help, she will get the illusion of control
   * Don't ask questions that start with "Waarom" unless you want the counterpart to defend a goal that serves you. "Waarom" is always an accusation.
   * Calibrate your questions to point your counterpart toward solving your problem. This will encourage them to spend energy looking for a solution
   * Bite your tongue. Avoind angy emotional reactions. Instead, ask a calibrated question
